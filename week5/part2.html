Exercises: Some questions for the video

  1. Who is Edward Tufte? (Go online and find his info), summarize in max 3 lines.

    Edward Tufte is an American statistician who is an expert in presentation of informational graphics.

  2. What is the "Lie Factor"?

    Tufte has tried to described the Lie Factor with the following formula: (size of effect shown in graphic)/(size of effect in data) => keep near 1.

  3. What's the idea behind the "Data-Inc ratio". Should it be maximized or minimized?

    Tufte has again tried to describe the Data-Inc ratio with a formular: (data ink)/(total ink used to print graphic) => maximize. In essence this means that one should try to say as much as possible with as little ink as possible. A example of where people fail to do so is when, for a very simple data set, the visulization is clutered with animations, colours, borders, legends, different font size, type and style.
